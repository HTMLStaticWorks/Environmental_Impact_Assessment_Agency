Sierra Nevada Wind Infrastructure Assessment

Home Projects Sierra Nevada Case Study

Project Overview

The Sierra Nevada Wind Infrastructure project is a 200MW clean energy generation farm spread across 1,200 acres of high-altitude grasslands. Designed to supply electricity to over 80,000 households, the construction required a comprehensive Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) to evaluate and offset potential ecological disruptions to local bird pathways and soil structures.

Key Environmental Challenges

The project site intercepts the migratory corridor of several protected raptor species, including the Golden Eagle. Additionally, heavy grading of steep slopes to build turbine foundations posed severe soil erosion and sediment wash-off risks to downstream trout-spawning creeks.

Assessment Scope

Envit’s interdisciplinary advisory team carried out a 12-month environmental audit encompassing:

  • Radar-based migratory trajectory tracking over four seasons.
  • Soil stability and core geological grading vulnerability mapping.
  • Acoustic dispersion modeling to map sound levels in nearby forest zones.
  • Hydro-geological runoff mapping to trace downstream creek water quality.

Mitigation Measures

To address the environmental challenges and secure permits, Envit engineered a comprehensive mitigation plan:

  • SCADA Automatic Camera Brakes: Installed automated camera-radar units that slow or stop turbines when raptors fly within a 500-meter safety zone.
  • Terraced Grading & Silt Buffers: Designed stepped construction trails combined with geotextile silt mats to retain 98% of displaced soil.
  • Acoustic Canopy Buffer: Positioned structural buffers and buffer tree corridors to absorb low-frequency turbine vibrations.

Approval Outcome

Our final Environmental Impact Statement (EIS) was submitted to the state energy board and federal land offices. Due to the high level of detail in our mitigation models and the integration of automated safety overrides, the project secured a Joint Record of Decision (ROD) and construction permits on the first filing cycle, bypassing any public interest litigation delays.
